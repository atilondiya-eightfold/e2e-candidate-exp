A

Linear playlist

One sequenced list, "up next" highlighted, "Resume" CTA at the bottom. Feels like Duolingo or a Coursera course. Removes choice: open the app → click resume → done. Hides dimension grouping (just shown as a small tag per row).

B

Grouped by weak dimension

Sections per dimension, with the topic chips you locked in for feedback at the top of each section. Per-section "Mock this →" CTA opens a focused mock just on that dimension. Completed sections collapse. Lets the candidate self-direct (which they'll want when they have a strong-area to refresh too).

H

Hybrid — "Up next" hero on top, grouped sections below

Take A's "Up next" highlighted row and pin it at the top as the single-CTA path for "just tell me what to do." Below it, B's grouped sections for the self-directed candidate. Best of both: zero-friction default with autonomy when wanted.

My recommendation: H (hybrid). Default path is dead simple ("resume"), but the grouped view lets a candidate jump straight to consistency when they know that's the area they want to drill. Reuses the chip vocabulary from gap report and feedback so the same "Quorum reads" / "Vector clocks" chips appear in all three places — strong cross-screen consistency.
