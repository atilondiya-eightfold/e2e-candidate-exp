Severity counts at top (high / medium / covered), then a clean ordered list. Each row: dimension name + severity badge + one-sentence "why this is a gap" pulled from the matching graph. Optimized for "tell me what to fix, in order."

B

Radar + topic clusters

Pentagon radar showing candidate (purple) vs. role expectation (dashed navy) at a glance. Below it, the weakest dimensions break out into topic chips — the same kind of chip you locked in for feedback. Visual hook + scannable next-steps.

H

Hybrid — severity counts + topic chips (no radar)

A's severity counts at top, B's topic-chip clusters below grouped by severity. Drops the radar (radar charts look impressive but candidates rarely understand them at a glance). Same chip vocabulary as the feedback screen, which means the candidate sees the same "Quorum reads" chip in gap report → in feedback → in study plan. Strong consistency cue.

My recommendation: H (hybrid). The radar is pretty but it's the wrong unit for "what should I study next" — chips are the unit. Reusing the chip vocabulary across gap report, feedback, and study plan makes the whole app feel like one coherent system instead of three separate screens.
